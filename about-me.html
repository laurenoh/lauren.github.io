About me

The plunge

My passion for UX Design slowly developed over the course of my life’s journey. All of these experiences have shaped me into the designer that I am today. A designer who is passionate about finding meaningful solutions for users by empathizing with them.

While working at an agency, I had opportunities to work with the Creative Directors. I worked on mini projects and had weekly review sessions. The entire process confirmed my passion as a creative. The agency’s creative environment gave me the push I needed to make a career change.

Making the jump from psychology to advertising and finally to design was a frightening yet rewarding decision. Each of the stops I took taught me important lessons and gave invaluable experiences that ultimately lead me to UX Design and became the building blocks of how I approach design.



Life experiences turned UX lessons

My psychology background helped me understand the behavioral and psychological processes of individuals - always asking interesting questions and attempting to answer them through all kinds of data-gathering methods.

Working in research labs taught me to hypothesize and communicate research findings to make informed decisions and build greater understanding of and empathy for the individuals.

As a former media planner, I used critical-thinking to plan out campaigns. I anticipated the needs and found solutions meanwhile, effectively managing my time working with various team members to make sure I delivered.



User-centric approach

UX design is a perfect combination of my passion for research, empathy and creativity. I strive to find user-centric solutions by putting myself in their shoes, and relating to users through my life experiences.

I design so that I can make the users’ lives easier by designing products that they can truly experience.
