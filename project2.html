CASE STUDY

PWR

SUMMARY

PWR app is a modern day, all-in-one workout app where you can easily access different workouts as well as other tools like meal planner, workout planner and community discussions that will encourage you to maintain a healthy lifestyle. The app can be accessed anytime and anywhere. It makes your life easier with an AI coach that sends you reminders to keep you on track. It also has customizable widgets for various data visualizations.

Overview


I did a quick survey about the fitness apps currently out in the market to uncover the problems and insights. Then, came up with the solutions to address those problems.

PROBLEMS

  • Inconvenience
  • Insufficient accountability/motivation
  • No coaching
  • Too many features
  • Confusing workout programs
  • No place to log weights, reps and sets
  • No activity record
  • Difficult meal planning


SOLUTIONS

  • Accessible anytime anywhere/various trainers
  • Private friend groups/community engagement
  • AI coach with reminders
  • Clean interface
  • Detailed step-by-step workout programs with a weekly workout planner
  • Notes - Weightlifting log
  • Data visualizations & Workout calendar

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

In order to determine the direction for PWR, I deep dived into the market and performed a competitive analysis and SWOT analysis on the competitors. In the survey, I asked the participants which type of fitness apps they used the most. Weight training, resistance and running were the top three. I conducted a competitive analysis on SWEAT, Nike Running and Nike Training Club mobile apps.

FINDINGS

From the analysis, I learned that a new fitness app with the following traits would be a great competitor:

  • 1. Accessible anytime and anywhere
  • 2. Budget friendly subscription options (flexible cost and plan)
  • 3. All-in-one app (HIIT, runs, weight, etc.)
  • 4. Intuitive and clear functionality
  • 5. Fast sync/upload
  • 6. Visually appealing and modern
  • 7. Customizable
  • 8. Convenient (short but effective)
  • 9. User engagement (social, community)
  • 10. Motivational (audio-guided, private messages with friends, badges - gamified)

USABILITY TESTING

Once I was done creating my wireframes, I brought it to life by incorporating the branding. With the initial hi-fi mockups, I performed three usability tests to get some feedback. Some feedback I had: change icon to send the correct message, update CTA button placement, and fix prototyping issues on the modal screen.

A & B TESTING

I did A/B testing using UsabilityHub when creating the various mockup versions.

Test 1 - Do you prefer the holographic onboarding pages or the pages with the images in the background? Why?

Out of 15 survey participants, 10 wanted to leave the current holographic background as is because it was more simple, uniform, consistent, less distracting, cleaner, etc. They said the images were too distracting while it added more character. 10/15 chose the holographic background making the test 90% statistically significant.

Test 2 - Should this page of the app be labeled as Coaching or Progress? (Both images are exactly the same EXCEPT the highlighted word in blue in the bottom nav bar) Why?

It was interesting to see that out of 10 survey participants, all 10 chose to change the page label from coaching to progress. Some of the reasons were: progress sounds more personal; the page is more about progress vs coaching; and it tells me where I am in my journey. 10/10 chose the progress label making the test 99% statistically significant.

FURTHER USER TESTING

Test 1 - Would you use a live coaching feature if it was added? You can have 1-on-1 live coaching session through the app on your phone. If you chose 'yes', how would it benefit you?

Out of 12 survey participants, 7 wanted the live coaching feature due to the following reasons: privacy, motivation, great for critiques, live feedback, personalized answers. You can find this within the chat feature with your coach. Press the camera icon which will allow you to have a live video stream or send photos to your coach to get critiques on your form. The first page looks exactly the same, but I added the second and third pages shown below.

Test 2 - The app has a reminder feature from your AI Coach. Would you like 'scheduling your workout time' feature to be added, so it can be sent as a reminder? If you chose 'yes', why?

Out of 13 survey participants, 10 wanted a scheduling feature that enables you to set a reminder to workout. Some of the reasons were: good accountability, good reminder for busy people, more commitment, set goals, habit maker, helps you prepare and relax. You can find the reminders section within your profile account.

Conclusion

WHAT I LEARNED


What worked?

Accessible anytime anywhere/various trainers
Private friend groups/community engagement
AI coach with reminders
Clean interface
Detailed step-by-step workout programs
Notes - Weightlifting log
Data visualizations
Workout calendar

What didn't

More specific AB testing on button labels, color, design, etc.

What were your doubts going into the project?

The market is saturated with fitness apps already. How would mine stand out? What would make it unique?

What surprised you the most?

Users really wanted the feature to log sets and reps + accountability and the option to skip onboarding.

What would you have done differently if given more time?

I would do focused testing on a variety of data visualization tools to see which is more effective on user retention.

What did you learn while doing this project?

Finding that balance for MVP - you want to include all basic features of a fitness app while keeping the app simple and easy-to-use. Also, don’t get too attached to a specific design, since you wanted it to look a certain way (ex: rounded corners = tacky).

How will you use that information in the future?

Learn to trust in the testing/research. And sometimes you may have a strong opinion about a design, but it won’t work ultimately. Testing is everything. Always.
